The problem

AI writes tests.
Nobody checks if they're any good.

Engineering teams are generating test suites at a scale no human team could match. The suite grows. Confidence doesn't.

False confidence

Green CI doesn't mean trustworthy tests

Your pipeline passes. Coverage metrics look fine. But your tests are checking the wrong things, in the wrong way, with selectors that break silently the next time your UI framework updates.

Convention drift

Standards erode every time an agent touches the suite

Page Object Model, naming conventions, fixture patterns — all carefully established, all quietly abandoned the moment AI-generated tests start arriving in bulk.

Maintenance black hole

The fix for bad tests is usually more bad tests

Teams respond to failing tests by prompting more agents. The quality problem compounds. Nobody owns it. Nobody has a baseline to measure it against.

No audit layer

No agreed standard to evaluate against

Framework best practices exist. Your team has conventions. But nothing is systematically checking whether your tests follow either — until something breaks in production.
